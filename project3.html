Still Humans

The start of a new tolerant society.

As studends at Hyper Island, we lead the future. We design, develop and build experiences powered by creative innovation and future technologies. This time we were challenged by Annie Thorell from Wrapp to find a solution to a problem that concerns us all.

We had 5 weeks, different backgrounds, a diverse set of skills and a lot of creativity in our hands. Our brief consisted in finding a mobile solution that can quickly make a difference and raise awareness on the ongoing global refugee crisis, by focusing on responsive web design and development.

  • Type: Web design, web development

  • Client: www.stillhumans.org

  • Date: 2015

The vision

We aim to fight dehumanization and tackle the stigma behind the refugees. Thatâ€™s why we launched Still Humans, a digital photography exhibition made by the refugees themselves. We would provide ten different individuals that have fled the Syrian war with cameras for seven days. Each of them would receive a kit cointaining information and the necessary tools. They would be able to take photos of anything they wanted and share with the world their point of view.

Vibrant colors

I wanted to play with an energizing and lively color palette. After looking at many different hues and combinations, I picked a set of three colours that communicated clear meaning, worked harmoniously, and provided the energy to express my own brand.

Sharp and focus

Since it is a website that highlights images, I wanted to keep the text content reduced but at the same time clear and sharp. That is why I wanted to give this website a lot of white space between images and descriptions. By using this typography I provide the importance that headlines need, always keeping a typographic contrast.

Start somewhere

In order to have have a clear vision of what I wanted this website to look like, I started sketching some pages. It was important to define the core features, such as the lightbox slideshow, and the parked features, such as the FAQ and blog, to not being overwhelmed by the project. Then I could begin the expansive ideation stage again, but within a narrower realm.
